Hi! I'm Jazzmyne! 👋

I'm a mixed methods researcher specializing in survey design and quantitative analysis. I was born and raised in Chicago, IL and am currently based in Seattle, WA.

My path into research started with data. Before becoming a researcher in graduate school, I discovered that I genuinely enjoyed collecting, cleaning, manipulating, and exploring data. I liked digging into large datasets and trying to make sense of complex information.

I originally got my start in market research as a Jr. Statistician, where I developed a strong foundation in statistical analysis, survey design, and sampling. It was there that I began learning not only what people liked, but what new products they might want and how they actually use the products they already have.

Eventually my curiosity led me to transition into UX research, where I could connect data with real user experiences and better understand how people interact with mobile products.

I've always been curious about why people like the products they use, what new products they want, and how they make decisions. Early in my career, I realized I enjoy digging into complex questions and uncovering patterns in the data.

Throughout my career, I've researched a wide range of topics, from finance and auto to social media, communication, and mobile games.

When I'm not conducting interviews or analyzing data, you can usually find me:

  • Watching Star Trek episodes 🖖
  • Learning chess on Duolingo ♟️
  • Completing puzzles 🧩
  • Trying new recipes and cuisines 🍲

Many of these hobbies reflect the same curiosity and strategic thinking that I bring to research, especially the challenge of connecting pieces and making sense of complex problems.
