Case Study

Benchmarking Video Calling Use

Measuring awareness, usage, and sentiment toward voice and video calling across a global landscape.

CompanyFacebook
TeamRemote Presence
MethodBrand Tracking Survey
My RoleUX Researcher
Participants25,000 Respondents

Background

Facebook, Messenger, and Instagram are social media platforms owned by Meta Platforms that allow billions of people around the world to connect and communicate online. Facebook focuses on social networking and communities, Messenger supports direct messaging and voice and video calling, and Instagram centers on photo and video sharing.

In 2020, Facebook launched Messenger Rooms, a group video calling feature that allowed up to 50 people to join a call without needing a Facebook account. By 2021, calling had become a central part of communication across Meta's messaging ecosystem, with more than 700 million people making voice and video calls each day across Messenger and WhatsApp. With this expansion, the team needed to understand product market fit for Messenger Rooms alongside other video calling products within the Facebook ecosystem.

Challenge

The team had an established tracking survey measuring messaging usage on Instagram and Messenger compared to other messaging apps. However, the survey did not include measures for voice or video calling, leaving a gap in our ability to understand how calling on Meta platforms compared to other communication apps.

Problem

The team needed to understand how voice and video calling on Meta platforms compared to other communication apps in the market. Specifically, we needed to measure awareness, usage, and sentiment toward voice and video calling.

My Role

I collaborated with the Lead Messenger and Instagram researchers to design the survey and partnered with an external vendor to field the study. I analyzed the results to identify insights that informed product strategy and growth opportunities for voice and video calling features.

Process

We conducted cognitive testing to ensure key survey measures were clear and easily understood. We then fielded a survey with 25,000 teens (13+) and adults across the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, the Philippines, and Vietnam.

The study was conducted with an external vendor and recruited a broad population of communication app users rather than only Facebook or Instagram users to avoid platform bias. The survey measured awareness, usage, and sentiment toward voice and video calling across major apps including Messenger, WhatsApp, Instagram, FaceTime, Zoom, and Discord. Initial analysis focused on users under 30, the primary target audience for calling products on Messenger and Instagram.

Example Survey Questions
  • Which apps do you use for voice and video calls?
  • How frequently do you make video calls on each app?
  • Which app do you use most often for video calls?
  • How would you rate the quality and reliability of calls across apps?

What We Learned

Messenger and Instagram had strong awareness among younger users, but they were not always the primary apps used for voice or video calling. Calling behavior was heavily influenced by platform ecosystems and use case context. For example, iPhone users frequently defaulted to FaceTime, while apps like Zoom were commonly used for scheduled group calls. Overall, Meta's calling products were competitive in the market but were often used alongside other communication apps rather than serving as the primary calling platform.

Impact

The research established a global baseline for voice and video calling and benchmarked Meta platforms against competitors. The findings informed product strategy and highlighted opportunities to improve adoption, positioning, and competitive standing of calling features on Messenger and Instagram, such as leaning into shared, collaborative hangout spaces.

Following this work, the survey was established as a recurring tracking study, first launched domestically in the U.S. and later expanded globally, and is now run twice annually to monitor trends over time.
